Governing Equations

The Navier-Stokes equations describe the motion of fluid substances such as liquids and gases. These equations are fundamental to fluid dynamics and are used to model weather, ocean currents, water flow in pipes, and air flow around wings, among many other applications.

Navier-Stokes Equations

The Navier-Stokes equations are expressed as:

∂u/∂t + (u · ∇)u = -∇p/ρ + ν∇²u + f
∇ · u = 0

Where:

  • u: Velocity field
  • t: Time
  • p: Pressure
  • ρ: Density
  • ν: Kinematic viscosity
  • f: External forces
