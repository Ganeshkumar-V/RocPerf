Governing Equations

The mass conservation equations of gas and particle phases are given by

$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\alpha_k \rho_k) + \nabla \cdot (\alpha_k \rho_k \mathbf{u}_k) = \dot{m}'''_{prop \rightarrow k} \tag{1} $$

where $\alpha_k$, $\rho_k$, and $\mathbf{u}_k$ are the volume fraction, density, and velocity of the $k^{\text{th}}$ phase, respectively. The source term due to mass transfer from propellant to $k^{\text{th}}$ phase is denoted as $\dot{m}'''_{prop \rightarrow k}$. The momentum conservation equations for the gas phase and particle phase are given by [10, 11].

$$ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\alpha_g \rho_g \mathbf{u}_g) + \nabla \cdot (\alpha_g \rho_g \mathbf{u}_g \mathbf{u}_g) = & -\alpha_g \nabla P + \nabla \cdot (\alpha_g \mathbf{\tau}_g) \\ & + \mathbf{M}_{prop \rightarrow g} + \mathbf{f}_{D,g} \end{aligned} \tag{2} $$
$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\alpha_p \rho_p \mathbf{u}_p) + \nabla \cdot (\alpha_p \rho_p \mathbf{u}_p \mathbf{u}_p) = -\alpha_p \nabla P + \mathbf{M}_{prop \rightarrow p} + \mathbf{f}_{D,p} \tag{3} $$

where $P$ is the common pressure experienced by all the phases, $\tau_k$ is the shear stress in the $k$th phase, and $f_{D,k}$ is the drag force experienced by the $k$th phase. The subscripts $g$ and $p$ refer to gas and particle phases, respectively. Note that the particle–particle interaction terms are not included in the above equations. The justification for this omission as well as the effect of particle–particle interactions on propulsive performance are discussed in Sec. V. Here $\mathbf{M}_{prop \rightarrow g}$ and $\mathbf{M}_{prop \rightarrow p}$ represent momentum source terms for the gas phase and particle phase, respectively, due to regression. The shear stress in the gas phase is modeled as follows:

$$ \tau_g = \mu_g (\nabla \mathbf{u}_g + \nabla \mathbf{u}_g^T) - \frac{2}{3} \mu_g (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}_g) \mathbf{I} \tag{4} $$

The energy conservation equations for the gas phase and particle phase considered are [10]

$$ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\alpha_g \rho_g h_g) + \nabla \cdot (\alpha_g \rho_g \mathbf{u}_g h_g) = & \nabla \cdot \left( \frac{\alpha_g \lambda_g}{C_{p,g}} \nabla h_g \right) + \alpha_g \frac{DP}{Dt} \\ & + \alpha_g \mathbf{\tau}_g : \nabla \mathbf{u}_g + H_{prop \rightarrow g} + \dot{q}'''_{g \rightarrow p} \end{aligned} \tag{5} $$
$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\alpha_p \rho_p h_p) + \nabla \cdot (\alpha_p \rho_p \mathbf{u}_p h_p) = \alpha_p \frac{DP}{Dt} + H_{prop \rightarrow p} + \dot{q}'''_{g \rightarrow p} \tag{6} $$
